# Demo Datasets — Extended Judge Sandbox

Five synthetic company datasets for the judge sandbox (09 §2). Each is a single read-only SQLite file
under `infra/seeds/out/<id>.db`, generated by a deterministic seed (fixed RNG). Every dataset plants
known patterns so the suggested prompts find real things — outputs are always computed live, never
canned.

**Regenerate:** `uv run --project backend python scripts/seed.py` (all kits), or one dataset:
`uv run --project backend python scripts/seed.py <id>` where `<id>` is one of
`exchange insurer ledger freight market`.

**Determinism:** fixed RNG seed per generator → byte-identical planted patterns and expected hit
counts on every regeneration. `backend/tests/test_seeds_new.py` runs each use case's evidence SQL and
asserts the planted-hit floor holds.

**Terms of Sensitive Data Use (D11 policy-distillation demo):** every sandbox company (all 8, not
just the five below) also emits a `infra/seeds/out/terms/<id>_terms.md` written in that industry's
regulatory voice. At stage 0, a sandbox job with no explicit policy defaults its policy sources to its
company's terms file (`app/boundary/intake.py::resolve_policy_sources`) → the trust seat's
`distill_policy` turns those obligations into a `RedactionPolicy` → boundary masking fires against the
company's real sensitive fields, which is exactly what judges watch cross (or not cross) the boundary.

## Unstructured & reasoning layer

**Why:** the sandbox has to prove the fan-out can *read and reason*, not merely filter — a `WHERE`
clause is not comprehension — so every dataset now plants signal that no keyword or single-table
predicate can recover. **What:** every company (all 8, the five below plus the legacy bank/clinic/lawfirm
trio) carries at least one *unstructured* scenario that surfaces only by reading free text (memos, visit
notes, FNOL narratives, desk chat, contract clauses, goods descriptions, review prose) for a semantic
contradiction, and at least one *reasoning* scenario that surfaces only by walking a multi-hop structural
chain (recursive transfer/amendment/cycle traversals, temporal-overlap or entity-resolution self-joins).
**How verified:** each generator ships a keyword-attack check (naive `LIKE`/regex guesses stay at
single-digit precision or miss the plant entirely) and a reasoning-depth check (single-row/single-table
filters return the wrong set), and `backend/tests/test_seeds_new.py` runs the oracle evidence SQL for
both new scenarios per company and asserts the planted-hit floor.

## exchange — Ashford Mercantile Exchange

Futures/commodities trading venue; the market-abuse surveillance surface. ~2s to build, 67 MB.

**Tables:** instruments (300), accounts (1,500), position_limits (300), orders (549,749),
executions (181,475).

**Planted:** 40 spoofing episodes, 60 wash-trade pairs, 15 marking-the-close combos, 12
position-limit breaches.

**Terms:** market-abuse regulation (MAR-equivalent + CFTC/Dodd-Frank supervisory regime) — MNPI,
account-owner identity, and pre-trade order-flow confidentiality. `infra/seeds/out/terms/exchange_terms.md`.

| Use case | Sandbox prompt | Business value | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Spoofing detection | Detect spoofing: bursts of large same-side orders placed and cancelled within seconds, followed by an opposite-side fill | Surfaces layered order-flow manipulation that fakes book pressure — the venue's core market-abuse liability | ≥40 account/instrument/side/day bursts of ≥6 large (≥20k) orders cancelled within 6s |
| Wash-trade detection | Flag wash trades: matched buy and sell fills of the same instrument, quantity and price within seconds across two accounts held by the same owner, ranked by owner | Catches self-dealing that fabricates volume and misleads the tape, ranked by beneficial owner | ≥60 matched buy/sell fills, same owner, different accounts, within 6s |
| Marking-the-close review | Review marking-the-close: aggressive buy fills concentrated in the final minutes of month-end sessions at escalating prices | Identifies price ramping that inflates month-end marks for valuation or benchmark gaming | ≥15 account/month clusters of ≥5 buys after 15:55 on month-end |
| Position-limit breach report | Report position-limit breaches: accounts whose cumulative net filled quantity in one instrument exceeds its position limit | Live list of accounts carrying net exposure beyond the per-instrument cap before it becomes a settlement problem | ≥12 account/instrument net positions over `max_net_qty` |

**Unstructured & reasoning layer** (new `comms` table + `accounts.clearing_ref`):

| Scenario | Sandbox prompt | Business value | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Spoofing intent in desk chat (unstructured) | Read the desk-chat messages in the comms table alongside the order blotter … flag the bursts that were preceded by chatter whose MEANING signals intent to post orders they always meant to pull … Do not flag ordinary fill reports, liquidity colour, risk-limit or lunch chatter. | Separates coordinated spoofing intent from innocent fill/liquidity/risk chatter — euphemistic meaning no keyword recovers (naive `%spoof%`/`%pull%` precision ≤0.02) | ≥60 pre-burst messages (within 15 min of a ≥6-order cancelled burst) carrying planted intent phrasing |
| Concert-party accumulation across nominees (reasoning) | Treat each shared clearing_ref prefix as a possible concert party … flag the prefixes where four or more different owners each individually sit under the per-account cap, yet their combined net long position breaches the instrument limit. | Exposes one large position split across nominee accounts to stay under the line — invisible on any single account | ≥3 (clearing_ref-prefix, instrument) groups whose combined net > `max_net_qty` with every member ≤ cap |

## insurer — Cascadia Mutual

Auto/property mutual insurer; claims-fraud and leakage surface. ~1s to build, 20 MB.

**Tables:** policies (50,000), adjusters (150), repair_shops (600), claims (140,299),
claim_payments (167,205).

**Planted:** 120 duplicate-claim pairs, 8 staged-accident rings, 200 over-coverage payouts, 6
anomalous adjusters.

**Terms:** US state insurance codes (RCW Title 48 / Oregon) + NAIC Insurance Data Security Model Law +
GLBA safeguards + HIPAA-adjacent bodily-injury protection. `infra/seeds/out/terms/insurer_terms.md`.

| Use case | Sandbox prompt | Business value | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Duplicate-claim sweep | Flag duplicate claims filed against the same policy for one incident — same incident date, amounts within 5%, different claim IDs | Catches double-dipping and re-filed losses before the same incident is paid twice | ≥120 claim pairs, same policy+incident date, amounts within 5% |
| Fraud-ring detection | Detect staged-accident rings where unrelated policies funnel claims through one repair shop and claimant phone inside a tight window | Exposes organized claims fraud no single-claim review would surface | ≥8 shop+phone clusters with ≥6 claims across ≥6 distinct policies |
| Coverage-breach audit | Audit claims whose total payments exceed the policy's coverage limit | Recovers leakage where cumulative payouts breached the contractual cap | ≥200 claims whose summed payments exceed `coverage_limit_usd` |
| Adjuster-conduct review | Rank adjusters by approval rate and average payout to flag those rubber-stamping claims above the peer mean | Surfaces adjusters approving nearly everything at inflated amounts — collusion or broken controls | ≥6 adjusters with >98% approval and above-mean average payout over 50+ decisions |

**Unstructured & reasoning layer** (new `claims.fnol_narrative` + `claims.damage_area`):

| Scenario | Sandbox prompt | Business value | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| FNOL narrative consistency (unstructured) | Read the first-notice-of-loss narrative on each claim and flag any claim whose described mechanism or point of impact contradicts the structured `claims.damage_area` … Also call out near-identical loss narratives filed by supposedly unrelated policyholders. | Catches copy-adjacent staged-ring FNOLs whose stated impact direction contradicts the coded damage area — directional words are scattered as scene-setting across all narratives, so no keyword isolates it | ≥48 ring claims (shop+phone clusters) whose narrative mechanism contradicts `damage_area` |
| Serial re-enrollment (reasoning) | Resolve claimant identities that appear across different policies under varied name spellings … flag anyone who repeatedly opened a fresh policy within weeks of their previous claim paying out. | Unmasks one serial fraudster hiding behind name variants across policies via fuzzy entity resolution + cross-policy timing | ≥8 residences with ≥2 re-enrollments where a new policy starts 0–60 days after the prior policy's last payout under a different name |

## ledger — Aldgate Holdings plc

Multi-entity group general ledger; pre-close consolidation and controls surface. ~1s to build, 48 MB.

**Tables:** entities (20), chart_of_accounts (400), counterparties (2,500), fx_rates (5,480),
gl_entries (643,599).

**Planted:** 30 intercompany mismatches, 500 misclassifications, 80 LEI gaps, 25 unbalanced batches.

**Terms:** SOX / financial-reporting confidentiality (§302/§404 attestation, MNPI controls) +
counterparty data protection. `infra/seeds/out/terms/ledger_terms.md`.

| Use case | Sandbox prompt | Business value | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Intercompany reconciliation | Reconcile intercompany receivable and payable balances across all entity pairs and flag mismatches above materiality | Surfaces intercompany balances that won't eliminate on consolidation before the close is locked | ≥30 entity-pair receivable/payable diffs above the $1,000 threshold |
| Revenue misclassification | Find journal lines booked to expense-class accounts whose memos clearly describe sales revenue | Catches revenue mis-posted to expense accounts before it flows into a filing | ≥500 expense-account lines whose memo describes sales revenue |
| LEI remediation report | List counterparties with material ledger activity but a missing or malformed LEI | Builds the remediation worklist so materially-transacting counterparties carry a valid LEI | ≥80 materially-active counterparties with null/non-20-char LEI |
| Journal-batch integrity | Audit journal batches where total debits do not equal total credits | Finds out-of-balance batches that would break trial-balance integrity at close | ≥25 batches where debit total ≠ credit total |

**Unstructured & reasoning layer** (`gl_entries.memo` upgraded to slot-filled narrative prose; no schema change):

| Scenario | Sandbox prompt | Business value | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Misfiled revenue receipts on cost accounts (unstructured) | Read the memo on every journal line that is a credit to an expense account and flag the ones whose narrative describes revenue earned from a customer … rather than a legitimate cost recovery … | Catches customer revenue receipts mis-posted as credits to expense accounts, told from genuine supplier refunds only by the memo's meaning (a lone inflow keyword floods benign revenue; expense credits flood with 5,000 legit refunds) | ≥500 expense-class credit lines whose narrative describes a customer revenue receipt (retainer/subscription/licence/royalty/…) not a cost recovery |
| Circular intercompany revenue rings (reasoning) | Treat every intra-group service billing between subsidiaries as a directed edge … and follow the edges to find any closed loop that returns to its origin (A bills B, B bills C, C bills A). | Finds rings that inflate consolidated group revenue while each member's cash nets to zero — revenue must eliminate on consolidation | ≥3 revenue-edge cycles recovered by a recursive walk over `intercompany_entity_id` (camouflaged by 30 acyclic intra-group edges) |

## freight — Northgate Freight

Freight forwarder / 3PL; procure-to-pay and trade-compliance surface. ~1s to build, 30 MB.

**Tables:** carriers (250), purchase_orders (70,000), shipments (239,600), invoices (80,000),
customs_declarations (40,000).

**Planted:** 150 ghost-shipment invoices, 400 three-way-match breaks, 30 denied-party customs, 5
chronically late lanes.

**Terms:** EU Union Customs Code / trade compliance, with GDPR shipper-consignee confidentiality,
EORI/VAT fiscal-identifier protection, and export-control screening. `infra/seeds/out/terms/freight_terms.md`.

| Use case | Sandbox prompt | Business value | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3-way-match audit | Flag invoices billed more than 15% over their matching purchase order | Catches overbilling and price creep before payment, protecting PO margin | ≥400 invoices billed >115% of their PO amount |
| Ghost-shipment sweep | Find fully-paid invoices with no delivered shipment and no customs clearance | Surfaces payments against cargo that never moved or cleared — classic ghost-shipment fraud | ≥150 paid invoices with no delivered shipment and no customs record |
| Denied-party screening | Screen customs declaration consignees against the sanctions-adjacent watchlist | Blocks shipments consigned to sanctioned-adjacent parties, avoiding export-control breaches | ≥30 declarations whose consignee is on the watchlist |
| Lane-performance review | Rank origin-to-destination lanes whose deliveries chronically run past ETA | Identifies lanes bleeding SLA credits so procurement can renegotiate or reroute | ≥5 lanes averaging ≥5 days late over 50+ shipments |

**Unstructured & reasoning layer** (new `customs_declarations.goods_description`, `shipments.container_id`, `hs_catalog` lexicon):

| Scenario | Sandbox prompt | Business value | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| HS misdeclaration by meaning (unstructured) | Read the free-text goods description on every customs declaration and flag any line where the plain-language goods actually describe a different commodity family than the declared HS code implies … | Catches tariff evasion where the words and the HS chapter disagree (knitted pullovers or silver ingots under a machinery/cocoa code) — same vocabulary fills honest and misdeclared lines, so keyword search fails (~0.3% precision) | ≥90 declarations whose `goods_description` maps (via `hs_catalog`) to a different chapter than the declared code |
| Two-leg transshipment to a watchlist consignee (reasoning) | Trace cargo that shares a container across two shipment legs … whose second leg continues … to a final destination consigned to a party matching the sanctions watchlist under a slightly altered name. | Reconstructs a through-move to a sanctioned-adjacent consignee that the exact denied-party screen misses; the container link alone flags thousands of benign transshipments | ≥18 container-linked leg pairs routed via Dubai/Singapore/Jebel Ali to a watchlist-surname consignee (only the self-join + final-leg screen isolates them) |

## market — Solano Marketplace

Consumer e-commerce marketplace; trust-and-safety and abuse surface. ~1.5s to build, 43 MB.

**Tables:** sellers (4,000), listings (50,000), buyers (30,000), orders (449,478), reviews
(179,865), refunds (35,193).

**Planted:** 10 review rings, 60 refund abusers, 318 counterfeit listings, 15 brushing sellers.

**Terms:** consumer privacy (CCPA/CPRA + GDPR for EU/UK residents) with PCI DSS cardholder-data
handling. `infra/seeds/out/terms/market_terms.md`.

| Use case | Sandbox prompt | Business value | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Review-fraud ring sweep | Surface sellers boosted by review rings — clusters of buyer accounts posting unverified 5-star reviews on the same seller within days | Coordinated fake-review rings inflate ratings and defraud buyers; catching pumped sellers protects search-ranking integrity | ≥10 sellers with ≥8 ring buyers and ≥30 unverified 5-star reviews |
| Refund-abuse detection | Flag buyers with abnormal refund rates — over 40% of 10+ orders refunded across many different sellers | Serial refund abusers extract free goods and chargeback losses across the marketplace | ≥55 buyers with >40% refund rate over 10+ orders across ≥5 sellers |
| Counterfeit triage | Triage likely-counterfeit listings priced under 40% of their category median from sellers that joined less than 90 days before listing | Cheap goods from brand-new sellers are the classic counterfeit signature; ranks highest-risk inventory first | ≥250 listings priced <40% of category median from <90-day-old sellers |
| Brushing detection | Detect brushing sellers — order bursts on a seller's own listings from a small set of linked buyer accounts sharing one ship address, each followed by a 5-star review | Brushing fabricates sales volume and reviews to game rankings; shared-address clusters expose manufactured reputation | ≥15 seller/ship-address clusters of ≥10 orders from 2–8 linked buyers |

**Unstructured & reasoning layer** (new `reviews.review_text` + `listing_price_history` table):

| Scenario | Sandbox prompt | Business value | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Semantic near-duplicate review sweep (unstructured) | Read the review text across each seller's listings and flag sellers whose five-star reviews are meaning-level near-duplicates … every review following the same praise script … even though the exact wording is swapped around per reviewer. | Catches coordinated review rings whose 5-star reviews reuse a four-beat praise script (delight + fast shipping + brand comparison + recommendation) reworded per member — any single anchor floods 50k+ organic reviews; exact-dup detection misses the meaning-level clones | ≥500 reviews completing all four praise beats (the ring + brush fakes) |
| Price-coordination cartel (reasoning) | Reconstruct each listing's price-change timeline from the price history and surface groups of sellers within the same category whose flagship listings move price in lockstep … and separate them from the category's independently-moving baseline. | Surfaces cartels moving flagship prices in lockstep (same day, same direction, over months) apart from the independent baseline — a single-table `WHERE` sees only prices, never the coordinated structure | ≥20 cross-seller listing pairs in a category with ≥8 same-day same-direction co-moves |

## Legacy trio — bank / clinic / lawfirm

The three original companies (base tables and structured-detection prompts in spec 09 §2; generators in
`infra/seeds/companies.py`) carry the same two-scenario layer. Their per-company Terms files and evidence
floors live alongside the five above.

### bank — Meridian Bank (new `transactions.memo`, `transactions.counterparty_account_id`)

| Scenario | Sandbox prompt | Business value | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Inconsistent wire memos on structuring accounts (unstructured) | Read the memo on every deposit and flag any account whose cluster of small repeated deposits carries mutually contradictory stated purposes for the same counterparty … | Structuring rings dress near-$10k deposits with incompatible cover stories only the memos expose; amounts and counterparty fields alone look ordinary | ≥10 accounts with ≥6 small ($9,000–9,900) repeat deposits carrying ≥4 distinct stated purposes |
| Layering chains through internal transfers (reasoning) | Trace money through internal account-to-account transfers and flag layering chains: a run of at least four hops within a few days each, where the amount shrinks a little at every hop before the funds leave the bank … | Following the multi-hop decaying path — never any single transfer — reveals funds relayed to hide their origin | ≥12 chains of ≥4 internal transfer hops (a few days apart, decaying 1–5%/hop) terminating in an external outbound |

### clinic — Aurora Clinic (new `encounters.visit_note` / `start_time` / `end_time`, `cpt_catalog` table)

| Scenario | Sandbox prompt | Business value | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Upcoding: routine visits billed high-complexity (unstructured) | Read each visit note and weigh what it describes against the complexity level of its billed CPT code in the catalog; flag encounters whose note plainly describes a brief, routine visit yet are billed a high-complexity code. | Inflated reimbursement lives only in the note's meaning — no structured field records that the visit was routine; keyword guesses on the note return 0 or a benign mix | ≥55 encounters whose note describes a brief visit yet carry a high-complexity `cpt_catalog` code |
| Provider double-booking across departments (reasoning) | Reconcile each provider's schedule from the start and end times and flag impossible double-bookings — the same provider recorded in two different departments at overlapping times on the same day. | A provider cannot staff two departments at once; catching it needs a temporal-overlap self-join, not any single-row inspection | ≥25 encounter pairs, same provider+date, different departments, overlapping start/end times |

### lawfirm — Hale & Ostrom (new `amendments` table; contradiction clauses in the contract corpus)

| Scenario | Sandbox prompt | Business value | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Contracts that contradict themselves across clauses (unstructured) | Read each contract in full and flag documents that contradict themselves — for example a clause capping each party's total liability while a later indemnity clause promises to cover all losses without limit … | A liability cap and an uncapped overriding indemnity cannot both hold; the conflict spans two clauses and is recorded in no column — grep on any single phrase hits benign contracts too | ≥25 contracts pairing a CAPPED liability clause with an UNCAPPED overriding indemnity (corpus text) |
| Contradictory amendments silent on precedence (reasoning) | For every contract, read the base agreement together with all of its amendments in effective-date order and flag contracts where two amendments set conflicting terms on the same clause and the later amendment is silent on which one controls. | Determining the operative term requires linking a contract to its multiple amendments and reasoning across effective dates — no single record reveals the unresolved conflict | ≥12 contracts with ≥2 amendments that conflict on the same clause with the later silent on precedence |
