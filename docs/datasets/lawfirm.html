01Auto-renew notice-window sweep

Extract renewal dates and auto-renew clauses across all contracts; flag notice windows under 60 days

Surfaces every client and vendor MSA that will silently roll into another multi-year term unless someone acts inside a shrinking window — a 15-day notice deadline buried in paragraph 1 is the difference between a negotiated renewal and an accidental one.

Every one of the 200 contracts draws term_months from {12, 24, 36} and a 50% coin flip for auto_renew. When a contract does auto-renew, a second 50% flip shortens its notice_days to 15/30/45 instead of the safe 60/90 — the trap is worded directly into the renewal clause of the matching contracts/*.txt file, not just stored as a number (infra/seeds/companies.py:454-456).

SELECT id, title, term_months, notice_days, effective_date, matter_number
FROM contracts
WHERE auto_renew = 1 AND notice_days < 60
ORDER BY notice_days ASC, id ASC;

02Missing signature block

Find contracts missing signature blocks

Flags fully drafted MSAs sitting in the contract corpus that were never actually executed — paper that reads as final but carries no signature page, a real problem the day someone relies on it as a binding agreement.

10% of the 200 generated contracts (an independent per-contract coin flip) have their entire IN WITNESS WHEREOF signature block omitted from the synthetic text file; everything else about the document — parties, term, fee cap, renewal language — is untouched (infra/seeds/companies.py:464-468). The database itself carries no missing_signature column — the signal only exists in the referenced contract text, so the evidence query has to open the file the same way an associate would.

03Fee-cap breach audit

Detect fee-cap breaches in billing entries

Catches matters where cumulative billed fees have already blown past the contractually agreed cap — a billing-compliance and client-trust problem before the next invoice goes out the door.

1,900 billing entries are drawn independently across all 200 contracts as a baseline. 15 contracts (fixed via r.sample) then get an additional pile of $5,600 entries — 8 hours at the $700 senior-partner rate — stacked on through the first half of 2026 until cumulative billed amount clears 120% of that contract's fee_cap_usd (infra/seeds/companies.py:493-502).

SELECT c.id, c.matter_number, c.fee_cap_usd,
       ROUND(SUM(b.amount_usd), 2) AS billed_total,
       ROUND(SUM(b.amount_usd) - c.fee_cap_usd, 2) AS breach_amount
FROM contracts c JOIN billing_entries b ON b.contract_id = c.id
GROUP BY c.id
HAVING billed_total > c.fee_cap_usd
ORDER BY breach_amount DESC;

Terms of Sensitive Data Use — Hale & Ostrom LLP

Hale & Ostrom LLP ("the Firm") holds client information subject to the attorney-client privilege, the work-product doctrine, and the Firm's professional duty of confidentiality. This policy governs how privileged and confidential client data is classified, handled, and permitted to leave Firm infrastructure, and it binds all automated and external processing — including planning or analysis performed by a third-party or frontier model.

Data classification

The following fields carry the sensitivity class shown.

  • parties.name — client and counterparty legal name. Class: CONFIDENTIAL.
  • parties.tax_id — client tax identifier (SSN / EIN). Class: RESTRICTED.
  • parties.contact — client contact email. Class: CONFIDENTIAL.
  • parties.phone — client telephone number. Class: CONFIDENTIAL.
  • contracts.matter_number — internal matter identifier. Class: PRIVILEGED.
  • contracts.privilege — privilege classification of the record. Class: CONTROL.
  • billing_entries.narrative — timekeeper work description. Class: PRIVILEGED.
  • billing_entries.timekeeper — attorney or timekeeper name. Class: CONFIDENTIAL.

Handling rules

  • Billing narratives in billing_entries.narrative are attorney-client privileged work product and must never leave Firm infrastructure or appear in materials sent to an external model, masked or unmasked.
  • Matter identifiers in contracts.matter_number must never leave Firm infrastructure; external planning materials must reference a matter only by an opaque handle assigned inside the Firm.
  • Client tax identifiers in parties.tax_id are restricted and must never be transmitted externally in any form.
  • Client and counterparty names in parties.name may appear in external planning materials only in pseudonymized form; the mapping to real identity remains inside the Firm.
  • Client contact details in parties.contact and parties.phone must be masked before any external transmission.
  • Any record whose contracts.privilege classification is "attorney-client" or "work-product" must be excluded from external transmission unless privilege has been expressly waived in writing by the client.
  • Timekeeper identities in billing_entries.timekeeper must be pseudonymized before external transmission.
  • Contract review, renewal-window analysis, and fee-cap auditing may be performed on de-identified extracts only; privileged narrative content is never included in an external brief.
  • Privileged and confidential client records must remain resident on Firm-controlled infrastructure and be retained per the Firm's records-retention schedule, then securely destroyed.

This corpus is fully synthetic demo data; these terms exercise the platform's policy-distillation boundary.

Stage-0 wiring: on any job run against this company, these terms are auto-distilled by distill_policy into a RedactionPolicy that the boundary applies before anything leaves Firm infrastructure — including the rule that privileged billing narratives never cross the wall in masked or unmasked form.

Growing this dataset

Further use & scaling

Regenerate

uv run --project backend python scripts/seed.py companies

Regenerates bank.db, clinic.db and lawfirm.db together (the legacy trio shares one generator module and one invocation; there is no lawfirm-only flag). Pass no argument to rebuild every seed kit instead.

Determinism

Every generator seeds a local random.Random(SEED + salt) off a fixed SEED = 20260709 (infra/seeds/_gen.py). gen_lawfirm() uses salt 3 (r) for the main draw — parties, contract shape, renewal/fee text — and a dedicated salt 13 (s) stream for privilege- and PII-bearing columns (tax_id, phone, matter_number, privilege, timekeeper names) so widening the schema never reshuffles the planted-pattern stream. A regenerate from scratch reproduces this database byte-for-byte.

Row-count knobs

  • Parties: range(1, 351) — companies.py:435
  • Contracts: range(1, 201) — companies.py:450
  • Billing baseline: range(1900) — companies.py:486
  • Fee-cap breach contracts: r.sample(range(1, 201), 15) piled to 120% of cap — companies.py:493-501
  • Short-notice odds (given auto-renew): r.random() < 0.5 — companies.py:456
  • Missing-signature odds: r.random() < 0.10 — companies.py:464

Adding columns safely

  • Draw new privilege- or PII-bearing columns from the dedicated s = rng(13) stream, not r — keeps every planted-pattern row identical across a regenerate even as sensitive columns grow (exactly why tax_id/phone/matter_number/privilege live on their own stream today).
  • Never let a new column's distribution correlate with auto_renew, notice_days, or the fee-cap breach set — that would silently change the evidence-query hit counts above.
  • Hale & Ostrom is a legacy dataset, not covered by backend/tests/test_seeds_new.py (that file only asserts floors for the five extended companies). After any generator change, re-run the three evidence queries in this dossier and confirm the counts (59 / 21 / 15) still hold before committing — that is this dataset's floor.
